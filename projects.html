Case Study · Robotics & Control

Arduino Uno Self Driving Car

Concept & Goal: To build a physical simulation vehicle chassis to model autonomous product routing, obstacle avoidance, and conveyor logistics inside supply chain nodes.

Implementation Details: Programmed an Arduino Uno controller to read real-time distances from an ultrasonic sensor. If an obstacle is detected within 30cm, the controller halts the chassis, swivels the sensor to assess clearing widths, and steers the H-bridge motor driver to navigate along the open path.

Outcome: Successfully demonstrated physical feedback loop concepts and sensor-integrated scheduling algorithms applied to automated logistics.

Case Study · Operations Research

Monte Carlo Petrol Pump Simulation

Concept & Goal: To evaluate customer queuing delays and dispenser utilization rates at a refueling station to optimize dispenser allocations.

Implementation Details: Programmed a Python simulation modeling vehicle arrival rates (using Poisson distributions) and service durations. Ran 10,000 Monte Carlo trials simulating bottlenecks, queue length expansions, and pump idle times under different capacity setups.

Outcome: Provided a capacity expansion strategy showing that adding a third pump reduces peak-hour queue build-ups by 35%, minimizing wait times with optimized capital cost.
